Requirements by country

Student visa rules for every destination

The key requirements, financial proof, work rights and post-study options for each country we place students in.

🇬🇧

United Kingdom

Student Route visa
  • Core requirements: CAS from a licensed sponsor, valid passport, English (IELTS/UKVI), TB test where required.
  • Financial proof: tuition for year 1 + living costs of £1,334/month (London) or £1,023/month (outside) for up to 9 months, held 28 consecutive days.
  • Health surcharge: IHS paid in advance; visa fee around £490.
  • Work: up to 20 hrs/week in term time, full-time in holidays.
  • After study: Graduate Route — 2 years (3 for PhD).

🇺🇸

United States

F-1 student visa
  • Core requirements: Form I-20 from an SEVP-approved school, valid passport, DS-160 confirmation, embassy interview.
  • Fees: SEVIS I-901 fee $350 + DS-160 application $185.
  • Financial proof: evidence of funds covering one full year of tuition and living costs.
  • Work: 20 hrs/week on-campus during term; full-time in breaks.
  • After study: OPT 12 months + 24-month STEM extension (36 months total).
Full USA guide →
🇨🇦

Canada

Study permit
  • Core requirements: Letter of Acceptance from a DLI, Provincial Attestation Letter (PAL), valid passport, medical exam.
  • Financial proof: GIC of CA$20,635 + first-year tuition; fee CA$150 + biometrics CA$85.
  • Work: up to 24 hrs/week off-campus during term; full-time in breaks.
  • After study: PGWP for up to 3 years — a recognised path to permanent residency.
Full Canada guide →
🇦🇺

Australia

Subclass 500 visa
  • Core requirements: Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE), Genuine Student statement, valid passport, English evidence.
  • Health cover: Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) for the full stay; visa fee around AUD 1,600.
  • Financial proof: funds for tuition, travel and living costs.
  • Work: up to 48 hours per fortnight while classes are in session.
  • After study: Temporary Graduate visa (485) — 2 to 4 years.
Full Australia guide →
🇪🇸

Spain

Student visa (Type D)
  • Core requirements: university acceptance, private health insurance, medical certificate, criminal-record check.
  • Financial proof: around €600/month (100% of IPREM) for the duration of stay; visa fee about €80.
  • Work: up to 30 hrs/week alongside study.
  • After study: 12-month job-search / stay-back permit.
Full Spain guide →
🇩🇪

Germany

National visa (Type D)
  • Core requirements: admission letter, valid passport, health insurance, proof of accommodation.
  • Financial proof: blocked account (Sperrkonto) of €11,904 for the year; visa fee around €75.
  • Work: 140 full days or 280 half days per year.
  • After study: 18-month job-seeker residence permit.
Full Germany guide →
🇭🇺

Hungary

Residence permit for study
  • Core requirements: letter of acceptance, valid passport, proof of accommodation, long-stay (Type D) entry.
  • Health cover: insurance with a minimum coverage of €50,000.
  • Financial proof: evidence of funds for tuition and living.
  • Work: up to 30 hrs/week during term.
  • After study: 9-month job-search residence permit.
Full Hungary guide →
🇵🇱

Poland

National visa (D) + TRP
  • Core requirements: acceptance letter, valid passport, health insurance, proof of accommodation and funds.
  • Residence: apply for a Temporary Residence Permit (TRP) for longer programmes.
  • Work: full-time students may work without a separate work permit.
  • After study: 9-month job-search permit.
Full Poland guide →
🇮🇹

Italy

Student visa (Type D)
  • Core requirements: enrolment confirmation, valid passport, health insurance, proof of accommodation.
  • Financial proof: around €460+/month of available funds; visa fee about €50.
  • Work: up to 20 hrs/week (max 1,000 hours/year).
  • After study: 12-month job-search permit.
Full Italy guide →
🇬🇪

Georgia

Visa-free / D3 study visa
  • Entry: citizens of 90+ countries enter visa-free for up to a year; others apply for a D3 immigration visa.
  • Core requirements: admission letter, valid passport, proof of funds — among the simplest processes in the region.
  • Medical degrees: universities are WHO-listed for international recognition.
  • After study: straightforward residence-permit renewals while enrolled.
Full Georgia guide →
🇲🇾

Malaysia

Student Pass (EMGS)
  • Core requirements: offer letter, EMGS approval, valid passport, medical screening.
  • Process: the university applies for your Student Pass through Education Malaysia Global Services (EMGS) before you travel.
  • Financial proof: evidence of funds for tuition and living.
  • Work: up to 20 hrs/week during semester breaks and holidays.
Full Malaysia guide →
