Where you'll live

Accommodation options explained

We match you to the option that fits your budget, age and study city — and handle the booking.

Most supportive

Homestay

  • Private room with a local host family
  • Half-board (breakfast & dinner) usually included
  • Best for younger students & first-time travellers
  • Fastest way to practise everyday English
Most social

Student residence

  • Private or shared room in a managed hall
  • On-site facilities, security and study spaces
  • Self-catering or meal-plan options
  • Surrounded by other international students
Most independent

Shared apartment

  • Room in a flat shared with other students
  • Full kitchen and living space
  • Often the best value for longer stays
  • Ideal for postgraduates & independent students

First two weeks

Your arrival checklist

We walk you through each of these so nothing is left to chance.

  • Confirm flight, luggage allowance & airport transfer
  • Move into your booked accommodation
  • Activate your local SIM & mobile data
  • Open a local bank account
  • Register with the university & collect your student ID
  • Complete any visa registration (e.g. BRP, residence registration)
  • Set up local transport card & learn your route to campus
  • Register with a doctor / activate health insurance
  • Attend orientation & meet your student support contact
  • Keep your counsellor's WhatsApp handy for anything else
